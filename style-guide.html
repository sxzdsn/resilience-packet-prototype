Pagination rules

Content-aware decisions are made at semantic block boundaries.

AUTO

Use the available page

Subsections flow into the remaining page area instead of moving as a complete unit to a fresh page.

SPLIT

Keep semantic units intact

Content splits between sentences, safe clause breaks (semicolon, colon, or em dash), list items, table rows, or other semantic blocks—not at arbitrary visual coordinates. A heading will not be left with only one line of paragraph copy when more content follows.

CONTINUE

Repeat context

When a Heading 3 subsection continues onto a new page, repeat its own title with a continued badge. Its Heading 2 parent does not repeat.

RESERVE

Protect the title band

Chapter titles occupy the top of their opening page. Subsequent content pages keep the same y = 136px content rail instead of drifting upward.

FORCE

Honor explicit breaks

Editors can force a fresh page before a subsection when the narrative or legal structure benefits from a deliberate transition.

WARN

Block unsafe export

An indivisible component taller than the 585px content area produces a fatal warning and disables PDF export until the content is corrected.
